About me

I am an experimental physicist at INESC TEC in Porto, Portugal, specializing in paraxial fluids of light as a platform for optical analogue simulations. My work spans advanced research in nonlinear optics, where I explore the development of experimental quantum simulators that emulate quantum fluid dynamics with high precision and tunability. Additionally, I focus on optical computing, implementing experimental analogues of extreme learning machines, reservoir computing, and diffractive neural networks. Recently, I have also ventured into the realm of experimental quantum optics, aiming to advance the capabilities of quantum sensing and information processing.

In addition to my primary focus on experimental work, I have a strong interest in theoretical physics and enjoy exploring new topics and mathematical models. I also possess robust programming skills, including developing numerical solvers for high-performance GPGPU supercomputing (C++, Python, Arrayfire, CUDA), conducting data analysis with Python, automating experiments with Arduinos, and applying machine learning and deep learning techniques.

What i'm doing

  • Paraxial Fluids of Light

    My primary research focuses on paraxial fluids of light, utilizing a photorefractive crystal as the nonlinear medium. This platform enables the exploration of intriguing phenomena, including optical analogues of superfluidity, two-dimensional quantum turbulence, topological effects, and even certain gravitational models.

  • Digital Feedback Loop

    I am also involved in developing a Digital Feedback Loop for paraxial fluids of light, which extends the effective emulation time of optical analogues by overcoming the limitations imposed by the fixed physical length of the medium.

  • Optical Computing

    My research also focuses on optical computing, exploring optical implementations of extreme learning machines, reservoir computing, and diffractive neural networks from both experimental and theoretical perspectives.

  • Quantum Optics

    Currently, I am exploring a new research direction in experimental quantum optics. My goal is to develop and utilize single-photon sources to study the Hong–Ou–Mandel effect, with applications in quantum sensing and quantum imaging. Furthermore, I aim to leverage these configurations to investigate advanced imaging techniques involving undetected photons.

Where I am

I am currently working in Porto at INESC TEC, specifically in the Centre for Applied Photonics, which is situated within the Faculty of Sciences at the University of Porto.
